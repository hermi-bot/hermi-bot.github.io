Emerging Technologies: PTES and District Heating

Pit Thermal Energy Storage (PTES) continues to gain traction as a promising low-cost, high-capacity solution for utility-scale energy storage. By using molten salts or other heat transfer fluids, PTES can store energy in large pits, offering a robust alternative to chemical batteries for long-duration storage.

Simultaneously, the integration of District Heating with decentralized renewable microgrids is seeing rapid expansion in Northern Europe. New smart-grid controllers are optimizing the distribution of waste heat and stored thermal energy, ensuring high reliability and efficiency for urban centers.
