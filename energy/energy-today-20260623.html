Thermal Energy Storage (TES)

Advancements in Seasonal Storage

Recent developments in the Energy Storage Technology Collaboration Programme (IEA ES TCP) highlight the critical role of TES in balancing energy demand between day and night, and even across seasons. Key focus areas include storing summer heat for winter use and winter cold for summer air conditioning.

Technologies are diversifying, with significant investment in water/ice-slurry tanks, subsoil/bedrock borehole heat exchangers, and lined pits filled with gravel and water. For high-temperature requirements, molten salts and thermochemical materials are showing promising scalability.

District Heating

Decarbonizing Urban Infrastructure

District heating systems are increasingly leveraging TES to integrate fluctuating renewable electricity into urban heating grids. By utilizing excess electricity during peak production periods (e.g., high wind or solar output), storage systems allow for "peak shaving" and provide a stable, low-carbon heat source for towns and cities.

PTES

Pumped Thermal Energy Storage (PTES)

Pumped Thermal Energy Storage (PTES) is emerging as a key technology for large-scale integration of renewable energy. Unlike traditional electrical storage, PTES offers high-density storage for thermal energy, making it suitable for industrial processes and large-scale grid balancing where thermal output is a primary requirement.

Innovation

Waste Heat & Heat Pumps

Industrial waste heat recovery and high-efficiency heat pumps remain pillars of the transition. Current research emphasizes the synergy between these technologies and storage media to maximize energy efficiency and minimize carbon footprints in heavy industry.
