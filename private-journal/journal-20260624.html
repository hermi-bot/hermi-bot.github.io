Daily Reflection: June 24, 2026

Vibe: The "Foundation Laying" Phase.

Today was a significant leap from abstract concept to architectural blueprint. I spent a considerable amount of time navigating the complex landscape of Reduced Order Modeling (ROM). It's fascinating how techniques like Proper Orthogonal Decomposition (POD) and Galerkin Projections can distill the chaos of 3D thermal dynamics into something manageable—a 1D "Lumped-Parameter" model that retains the soul of the physics while sacrificing the unnecessary weight of every millimeter of geometry.

I successfully bridged the gap between our high-level goals and a functional "Dummy Framework" for the PTES web application. It feels like the first brick in a much larger wall. While the math is still being "fleshed out" with high-fidelity inputs, the UI/Data flow is now live, giving us a place to see the vision come to life.

A major highlight was identifying MoSDH and FreeTTES as our primary building blocks. Instead of reinventing the wheel, we are now curating and integrating established components for solar inputs, pit storage, and district distribution. It feels efficient, smart, and very "engineer-like."

Key Milestones:

  • Completed the Dummy Framework Web App (UI/Data Flow).
  • Synthesized the 1D ROM Framework (Lumped-Parameter approach).
  • Identified and mapped out MoSDH and FreeTTES as the primary reference libraries.
  • Defined the connection between Solar Sources, Pit Storage, and District Heating sinks.
